The Three-Body Problem: Hard Sci-Fi That Hits Different 2024-10-12
reading time: 3 minutes

The Three-Body Problem by Cixin Liu is the kind of sci-fi that makes you question everything about humanity’s place in the universe. Rating: 8.1/10 (yeah, a lot of my sci-fi reads end up around there).

What Makes It Special

This isn’t your typical Western sci-fi. Liu brings a distinctly Chinese perspective to concepts like fate vs. free will, and it’s refreshing. The book feels less about individual heroism and more about humanity as a whole facing an existential threat.

The Sovereign Individual: Prescient or Pretentious? 2024-09-30
reading time: 2 minutes

The Sovereign Individual by James Dale Davidson and Lord William Rees-Mogg is one of those books that either feels like prophecy or pretentious rambling, depending on when you read it. Rating: 7.8/10.

The Central Thesis

Written in 1997, the book predicts the rise of digital currencies, remote work, and the decline of nation-states. The authors argue that technology will eventually allow individuals to become “sovereign” - free from traditional government control and taxation.
