The Sovereign Individual: Prescient or Pretentious? 2024-09-30
reading time: 2 minutes

The Sovereign Individual by James Dale Davidson and Lord William Rees-Mogg is one of those books that either feels like prophecy or pretentious rambling, depending on when you read it. Rating: 7.8/10.

The Central Thesis

Written in 1997, the book predicts the rise of digital currencies, remote work, and the decline of nation-states. The authors argue that technology will eventually allow individuals to become “sovereign” - free from traditional government control and taxation.
